- At the end of the day, AI is essentially a created person endowed with world-class knowledge.
- Factors influencing innovation: knowledge, personality, and the things one encounters. Personalities are endlessly varied, as are the events people encounter; different personalities combined with different experiences mean the feelings and ideas they trigger are never quite the same.
- For an AI, no matter how it is trained, as long as its personality remains singular — or the personality it applies when handling tasks is singular — won't its capacity for innovation be locked within a fixed framework?
- Moderate people do moderate things; radical people do radical things. If a person always remains moderate in how they conduct themselves, won't what they feel and what they gain also remain permanently moderate?
- How many permutations of human personality exist? How large is the gap in innovative potential among all these combinations? Extreme cases like Billy Milligan's 24 personalities do exist — but if we wanted to give AI multiple personalities too… I don't think that's realistic. For the sake of human safety, AI must remain moderate; it cannot be given a radical personality. Perhaps it could be kept radical in certain domains, but corresponding constraints would have to be imposed.
