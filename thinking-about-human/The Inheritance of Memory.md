If I spend my entire life ceaselessly repeating a certain thing, a certain phrase, will these be passed down to my descendants? Will they find themselves inexplicably dwelling on something, inexplicably persisting in something?

Perhaps memory itself cannot be inherited, but could the neural pathways that carry those memories be? The non-inheritability of memory may be merely a surface-level appearance. The real reason behind it could be that while the neural pathways carrying memory are indeed inherited, the information encoded along those pathways is different.

Perhaps it's like large models in AI — we inherit our parents' parameters, but not their data. We start accumulating data from zero and begin our own journey of parameter fine-tuning.
